Bald Eagle

The bald eagle is a North American bird of prey. A centuries-long cultural and religious icon for Native Americans and the national emblem of the United States, this mighty raptor has a wingspan of 5.5-8 feet and a height of 28-38 inches. They are a carnivorous species whose diet consists mainly of fish, but have been known to eat small mammals such as rabbits, squirrels and even young deer whenever fish populations fall scarce. Despite their name, bald eagles are actually not bald. Their name derives from an old translation of "piebald", meaning "white-headed".
