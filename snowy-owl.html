Snowy Owl

Native to the Arctic regions of both North America and the Palearctic, snowy owls are one of the largest species of owl and the only owl with mainly white plumage. Unlike other owls that sleep during the day and hunt at night, snowy owls are most active during the day, hunting mainly small mammals and northerly water birds. An opportunistic hunter, they will also take advantage of carrion if the need necessitates. Snowy owls are classified as vulnerable by the International Union for Conservation of Nature with fewer than 500,000 snowy owls left in the world. Though this number may seem large, it is quite small for birds.
