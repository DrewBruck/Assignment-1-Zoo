Gorilla

The largest living primates, Gorillas dwell in Sub-Saharan Africa amongst a vast range of elevations. They are herbivorous and are the closest living relatives to humans after chimpanzees and bonobos. Depending on the factors being compared, they have a 95-99% DNA similarity to humans. The genus “Gorilla” is divided into two species, the eastern gorilla and western gorilla, both of which are listed as critically endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature. Gorillas tend to live 35–40 years in the wild.
